Asthma

What is Asthma?

Asthma is a chronic condition that affects the airways of the lungs, making breathing difficult.

Symptoms

  • Shortness of breath
  • Wheezing
  • Chest tightness
  • Coughing
  • Difficulty breathing

Causes

  • Allergies
  • Air pollution
  • Respiratory infections
  • Genetic factors

Risk Factors

  • Family history of asthma
  • Exposure to smoke
  • Allergies
  • Air pollution

What to Eat

  • Fresh fruits
  • Vegetables
  • Whole grains
  • Foods rich in vitamins

What to Avoid

  • Smoking
  • Dust exposure
  • Strong perfumes
  • Known allergy triggers

Exercise

Light exercise such as walking, swimming and breathing exercises may help improve lung function.

Lifestyle Changes

  • Avoid asthma triggers
  • Maintain a healthy weight
  • Follow prescribed treatment
  • Keep indoor air clean

Complications

  • Severe asthma attacks
  • Sleep disturbances
  • Reduced physical activity
  • Breathing difficulties

Prevention

Avoiding triggers, maintaining a healthy lifestyle and following medical advice can help manage asthma.

When to Visit a Doctor

Consult a doctor if you experience frequent breathing difficulties, wheezing or worsening asthma symptoms.

FAQ

Can asthma be cured?
Asthma cannot usually be cured, but it can be effectively managed with proper treatment.

Can exercise help?
Yes, appropriate exercise can improve overall lung health and fitness.
