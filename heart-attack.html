Heart Attack Awareness

What is a Heart Attack?

A heart attack occurs when blood flow to a part of the heart is blocked, causing damage to the heart muscle.

Symptoms

  • Chest pain or pressure
  • Shortness of breath
  • Pain in the arm, neck or jaw
  • Nausea
  • Cold sweating

Causes

  • Blocked coronary arteries
  • High cholesterol
  • High blood pressure
  • Smoking

Risk Factors

  • Family history of heart disease
  • Diabetes
  • Obesity
  • Lack of physical activity

What to Eat

  • Fresh fruits
  • Vegetables
  • Whole grains
  • Lean protein

What to Avoid

  • Fried foods
  • Processed foods
  • Excess salt
  • Sugary drinks

Exercise

Regular walking, cycling and doctor-approved exercise can improve heart health.

Lifestyle Changes

  • Quit smoking
  • Maintain a healthy weight
  • Manage stress
  • Exercise regularly

Complications

  • Heart failure
  • Irregular heartbeat
  • Cardiac arrest
  • Reduced heart function

Prevention

Maintaining a healthy lifestyle, controlling blood pressure and cholesterol levels can reduce the risk of a heart attack.

When to Visit a Doctor

Seek immediate medical attention if you experience chest pain, shortness of breath, sweating or pain spreading to the arm, neck or jaw.

FAQ

Is a heart attack an emergency?
Yes, a heart attack is a medical emergency and requires immediate treatment.

Can heart attacks be prevented?
Yes, healthy lifestyle choices can significantly reduce the risk.
