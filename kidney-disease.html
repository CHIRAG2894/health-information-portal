Kidney Disease

What is Kidney Disease?

Kidney disease occurs when the kidneys are damaged and cannot effectively filter waste and excess fluids from the blood.

Symptoms

  • Swelling in feet and ankles
  • Fatigue
  • Frequent urination
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Loss of appetite

Causes

  • Diabetes
  • High blood pressure
  • Kidney infections
  • Genetic conditions

Risk Factors

  • Family history of kidney disease
  • Diabetes
  • High blood pressure
  • Older age

What to Eat

  • Fresh fruits
  • Vegetables
  • Whole grains
  • Doctor-recommended kidney-friendly foods

What to Avoid

  • Excess salt
  • Processed foods
  • Sugary drinks
  • Excessive protein intake if advised by a doctor

Exercise

Walking and light physical activity can help improve overall health and support kidney function.

Lifestyle Changes

  • Control blood pressure
  • Manage diabetes
  • Stay hydrated
  • Follow medical advice

Complications

  • Kidney failure
  • Heart disease
  • High blood pressure
  • Anemia

Prevention

Maintaining healthy blood pressure, controlling diabetes and following a healthy lifestyle can help prevent kidney disease.

When to Visit a Doctor

Consult a doctor if you experience swelling, changes in urination, fatigue or persistent kidney-related symptoms.

FAQ

Can kidney disease be treated?
Treatment depends on the cause and stage of kidney disease, but early management can slow progression.

Can diet help?
Yes, a kidney-friendly diet can help support kidney health and reduce complications.
