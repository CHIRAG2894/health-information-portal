High Blood Pressure (Hypertension)

What is Hypertension?

Hypertension is a condition in which blood pressure remains consistently higher than normal.

Symptoms

  • Headache
  • Dizziness
  • Shortness of breath
  • Chest pain
  • Blurred vision

Causes

  • High salt intake
  • Obesity
  • Lack of physical activity
  • Stress

Risk Factors

  • Family history of hypertension
  • Being overweight
  • Smoking
  • Lack of exercise

What to Eat

  • Fresh fruits
  • Vegetables
  • Whole grains
  • Low-fat dairy products

What to Avoid

  • Excess salt
  • Processed foods
  • Sugary drinks
  • Excess alcohol

Exercise

Walking, jogging and regular physical activity can help control blood pressure.

Lifestyle Changes

  • Maintain a healthy weight
  • Exercise regularly
  • Reduce salt intake
  • Manage stress

Complications

  • Heart disease
  • Stroke
  • Kidney disease
  • Vision problems

Prevention

A healthy diet, regular exercise and maintaining a healthy weight can help prevent hypertension.

When to Visit a Doctor

Consult a doctor if you frequently experience headaches, dizziness or high blood pressure readings.

FAQ

Can hypertension be cured?
Hypertension can usually be controlled with lifestyle changes and medication.

Can exercise help?
Yes, regular exercise helps lower blood pressure.
