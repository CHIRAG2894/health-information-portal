High Cholesterol

What is High Cholesterol?

High cholesterol is a condition in which there is too much cholesterol in the blood, increasing the risk of heart disease and stroke.

Symptoms

  • Often has no symptoms
  • Chest pain in severe cases
  • Fatigue
  • Shortness of breath
  • Heart-related complications

Causes

  • Unhealthy diet
  • Lack of physical activity
  • Obesity
  • Genetic factors

Risk Factors

  • Family history
  • Smoking
  • Diabetes
  • Being overweight

What to Eat

  • Fruits
  • Vegetables
  • Whole grains
  • Healthy fats such as nuts and seeds

What to Avoid

  • Fried foods
  • Processed foods
  • Trans fats
  • Excess sugary foods

Exercise

Walking, jogging, cycling and regular physical activity can help improve cholesterol levels.

Lifestyle Changes

  • Maintain a healthy weight
  • Exercise regularly
  • Quit smoking
  • Eat a heart-healthy diet

Complications

  • Heart disease
  • Heart attack
  • Stroke
  • Blocked arteries

Prevention

Eating a balanced diet, exercising regularly and maintaining a healthy lifestyle can help prevent high cholesterol.

When to Visit a Doctor

Consult a doctor if your cholesterol levels are high or if you have risk factors for heart disease.

FAQ

Can high cholesterol be controlled?
Yes, high cholesterol can often be controlled with diet, exercise and medication if needed.

Can exercise help?
Yes, regular exercise can improve cholesterol levels and heart health.
