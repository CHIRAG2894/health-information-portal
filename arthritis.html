Arthritis

What is Arthritis?

Arthritis is a condition that causes inflammation, pain and stiffness in one or more joints.

Symptoms

  • Joint pain
  • Joint stiffness
  • Swelling
  • Reduced range of motion
  • Joint tenderness

Causes

  • Age-related wear and tear
  • Autoimmune disorders
  • Joint injuries
  • Genetic factors

Risk Factors

  • Older age
  • Family history of arthritis
  • Previous joint injuries
  • Obesity

What to Eat

  • Fresh fruits
  • Vegetables
  • Whole grains
  • Foods rich in omega-3 fatty acids

What to Avoid

  • Highly processed foods
  • Excess sugar
  • Excess fried foods
  • Unhealthy fats

Exercise

Gentle exercises such as walking, stretching and swimming can help improve joint flexibility and reduce stiffness.

Lifestyle Changes

  • Maintain a healthy weight
  • Stay physically active
  • Protect joints from injury
  • Follow medical advice

Complications

  • Chronic pain
  • Joint deformity
  • Reduced mobility
  • Difficulty performing daily activities

Prevention

Maintaining a healthy lifestyle, staying active and protecting joints may help reduce the risk of certain types of arthritis.

When to Visit a Doctor

Consult a doctor if you experience persistent joint pain, swelling or stiffness that affects daily activities.

FAQ

Can arthritis be cured?
Arthritis usually cannot be cured, but symptoms can often be managed with proper treatment and lifestyle changes.

Can exercise help?
Yes, regular low-impact exercise can improve joint function and reduce stiffness.
