Thyroid Disorder

What is Thyroid Disorder?

Thyroid disorders occur when the thyroid gland produces too much or too little thyroid hormone, affecting many body functions.

Symptoms

  • Fatigue
  • Weight gain or weight loss
  • Mood changes
  • Hair loss
  • Changes in heart rate

Causes

  • Autoimmune diseases
  • Iodine imbalance
  • Genetic factors
  • Thyroid gland problems

Risk Factors

  • Family history of thyroid disease
  • Female gender
  • Autoimmune conditions
  • Increasing age

What to Eat

  • Fruits
  • Vegetables
  • Whole grains
  • Iodine-rich foods as advised by a doctor

What to Avoid

  • Excess processed foods
  • Excess sugary foods
  • Smoking
  • Unhealthy eating habits

Exercise

Walking, yoga and regular physical activity can help improve overall health and energy levels.

Lifestyle Changes

  • Take medications as prescribed
  • Maintain a balanced diet
  • Exercise regularly
  • Manage stress

Complications

  • Heart problems
  • Weight issues
  • Fertility problems
  • Mental health concerns

Prevention

Regular checkups and maintaining a healthy lifestyle may help detect and manage thyroid disorders early.

When to Visit a Doctor

Consult a doctor if you experience unexplained weight changes, fatigue, hair loss or changes in heart rate.

FAQ

Can thyroid disorders be treated?
Yes, many thyroid disorders can be effectively managed with medication and regular monitoring.

Can diet help?
Yes, a balanced diet can support thyroid health when combined with proper medical treatment.
